# HAIAMM Certified Assessor (HCA) Program
## Training and Certification for Assessment Partners

**Version:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** February 2026

---

## Program Overview

The HAIAMM Certified Assessor (HCA) program trains and certifies individuals to conduct official HAIAMM assessments. This enables consulting firms, MSSPs, and independent practitioners to deliver HAIAMM certification services.

### Value Proposition

| For Assessors | For Their Clients |
|---------------|-------------------|
| New revenue stream in AI security | Access to certified local expertise |
| Differentiated service offering | Faster assessment scheduling |
| Access to HAIAMM methodology | Consistent quality assurance |
| Ongoing training and updates | Regulatory-aligned assessments |
| Lead referrals from HAI-Security | Recognized certification |

---

## Certification Tiers

### HCA Associate

**For:** Entry-level practitioners, internal assessors
**Prerequisites:** 3+ years security experience
**Scope:** Can conduct Tier 1 (Foundations) assessments under supervision

### HCA Professional

**For:** Experienced consultants, lead assessors
**Prerequisites:** 5+ years security experience, HCA Associate or equivalent
**Scope:** Can lead Tier 1 and Tier 2 assessments independently

### HCA Master

**For:** Senior practitioners, quality reviewers
**Prerequisites:** 8+ years security experience, 10+ assessments completed
**Scope:** Can lead all tiers, train other assessors, perform quality reviews

## Certification Requirements

### HCA Associate

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Experience** | 3+ years in security, risk, or compliance |
| **Training** | Complete HCA Foundations Course (16 hours) |
| **Exam** | Pass HCA Associate Exam (70% minimum) |
| **Practical** | Shadow 1 assessment with HCA Professional |
| **Background** | Professional reference check |

**Investment:** $2,500 (includes training, exam, 1 year credential)

### HCA Professional

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Experience** | 5+ years in security, risk, or compliance |
| **Prerequisite** | HCA Associate OR equivalent experience waiver |
| **Training** | Complete HCA Professional Course (24 hours) |
| **Exam** | Pass HCA Professional Exam (75% minimum) |
| **Practical** | Lead 2 assessments with quality review |
| **Background** | Professional reference check |

**Investment:** $5,000 (includes training, exam, 1 year credential)

### HCA Master

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Experience** | 8+ years in security/AI, management experience |
| **Prerequisite** | HCA Professional for 2+ years |
| **Assessments** | 10+ completed assessments as lead |
| **Training** | Complete HCA Master Course (16 hours) |
| **Exam** | Pass HCA Master Exam (80% minimum) |
| **Practical** | Quality review 3 assessments |
| **Interview** | Panel interview with HAI-Security |

**Investment:** $7,500 (includes training, exam, 1 year credential)

## Training Curriculum

### HCA Foundations Course (16 hours)

**Module 1: HAIAMM Framework (4 hours)**
- History and purpose of HAIAMM
- The 12 practices and 4 functions
- The 6 domains
- Maturity levels (L1, L2, L3)
- Critical AI Risks (EA, AGH, TM, RA)

**Module 2: Assessment Methodology (4 hours)**
- Assessment lifecycle
- Evidence collection techniques
- Stakeholder interview skills
- Documentation review process
- Scoring methodology

**Module 3: Reporting and Certification (4 hours)**
- Report writing standards
- Gap analysis techniques
- Remediation roadmap development
- Certification criteria and decision-making
- Client communication

**Module 4: Practical Exercises (4 hours)**
- Mock interview exercises
- Evidence evaluation scenarios
- Scoring calibration exercises
- Report writing practice

### HCA Professional Course (24 hours)

*Includes all Foundations content plus:*

**Module 5: Advanced Assessment (4 hours)**
- Complex environment assessment
- Multi-domain evaluation
- Critical AI Risks deep-dive
- Regulatory mapping (EU AI Act, HIPAA, SEC)

**Module 6: Engagement Management (4 hours)**
- Scoping and pricing
- SOW development
- Stakeholder management
- Handling difficult situations
- Quality assurance

### HCA Master Course (16 hours)

**Module 7: Quality and Calibration (8 hours)**
- Assessment quality review process
- Calibration methodology
- Assessor coaching and feedback
- Continuous improvement

**Module 8: Program Development (8 hours)**
- Building an assessment practice
- Training delivery skills
- Thought leadership development
- Advanced regulatory topics

## Examination Details

### Exam Format

| Level | Questions | Duration | Passing Score |
|-------|-----------|----------|---------------|
| Associate | 75 multiple choice | 2 hours | 70% |
| Professional | 100 MC + 2 case studies | 3 hours | 75% |
| Master | 50 MC + 3 case studies + oral | 4 hours | 80% |

### Exam Topics

**Associate Exam:**
- HAIAMM framework knowledge (40%)
- Assessment methodology (30%)
- Scoring and reporting (30%)

**Professional Exam:**
- Framework deep knowledge (25%)
- Assessment scenarios (35%)
- Regulatory alignment (20%)
- Engagement management (20%)

**Master Exam:**
- Advanced framework (20%)
- Complex scenarios (30%)
- Quality assurance (25%)
- Program leadership (25%)

### Retake Policy

- First retake: Free (within 30 days)
- Second retake: $250 (within 60 days)
- Third attempt: Requires additional training

## Quality Assurance

### Assessment Quality Standards

All assessments subject to:

1. **Methodology Compliance**
   - Evidence-based findings
   - Proper scoring application
   - Complete documentation

2. **Report Quality**
   - Professional writing
   - Accurate findings
   - Actionable recommendations

3. **Client Satisfaction**
   - Post-assessment survey (target: >4.5/5)
   - Issue resolution within 5 days

### Quality Review Process

- Random sampling: 20% of all assessments
- Full review for new assessors (first 3 assessments)
- Triggered review for any client complaint

### Non-Conformance

| Severity | Example | Action |
|----------|---------|--------|
| Minor | Documentation gaps | Coaching, remediation |
| Major | Scoring errors | Supervised reassessment |
| Critical | Falsified evidence | Credential revocation |

## Code of Ethics

All HCA credential holders agree to:

1. **Integrity**
   - Conduct assessments honestly and objectively
   - Report findings accurately without bias
   - Disclose conflicts of interest

2. **Confidentiality**
   - Protect all client information
   - Never share assessment details without authorization
   - Secure all evidence and documentation

3. **Competence**
   - Only perform assessments within qualifications
   - Maintain current knowledge
   - Seek guidance when uncertain

4. **Professionalism**
   - Represent HAIAMM accurately
   - Communicate respectfully
   - Honor commitments

### Ethics Violations

Violations may result in:
- Warning and remediation
- Suspension of credential
- Revocation of credential
- Removal from partner program

## Application Process

### Individual Certification

1. **Apply Online**
   - Submit application at haiamm.io/assessor
   - Provide resume and references
   - Pay application fee ($100)

2. **Eligibility Review**
   - Experience verification (5 business days)
   - Reference check
   - Approval notification

3. **Complete Training**
   - Register for course
   - Complete all modules
   - Pass knowledge checks

4. **Pass Examination**
   - Schedule exam
   - Complete exam
   - Receive results (within 48 hours)

5. **Practical Requirement**
   - Complete required assessments
   - Submit for quality review
   - Receive certification

### Partner Application

1. **Express Interest**
   - Contact partners@hai-security.io
   - Provide firm overview

2. **Qualification Review**
   - Confirm HCA staff requirements
   - Review firm credentials
   - Discuss partnership tier

3. **Agreement Execution**
   - Sign partner agreement
   - Pay annual fee
   - Complete onboarding

4. **Onboarding**
   - Partner portal access
   - Methodology training
   - Quality standards review
   - Launch planning
